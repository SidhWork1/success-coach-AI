Build milestones

Nine milestones across six phases. Each milestone builds on the previous one. At every milestone you have something working that you can test. What follows is what completion looks like at each step.

Phase 1
Student can have a real conversation
M1 Basic conversation
Completion
  • Student sends a message and gets a relevant, coherent response back
M2 Student data in the conversation
Completion
  • Response contains the student's actual subject scores, attendance percentage and upcoming exam dates
  • Response highlights anything that needs attention — a dropping score, low attendance, an exam coming up soon
M3 Questions answered from knowledge base
Completion
  • Student asks something about what they are studying and gets an answer from the knowledge base
  • Answer is accurate and specific to the course content
Phase 2
System remembers the student
M4 Session is stored after it ends
Completion
  • When a session ends, what the student shared is captured and stored persistently
  • Nothing is lost between sessions
M5 AI knows the student's history
Completion
  • When the same student starts a new session, the AI already knows their history
  • Two types of memory are maintained separately
  • Factual memory — stress triggers, what has helped, recurring patterns — shapes how the AI responds in conversation
  • Session summaries — what was discussed, what was decided — used when the coach asks for a briefing
  • A student on their fifth session gets a meaningfully different response than a student on their first
Phase 3
Problems get surfaced automatically
M6 Signals detected and coach alerted
Completion
  • A signal is written after every session capturing what was concerning
  • Signal has a severity — how serious the issue is — and an urgency — whether the coach needs to act today or can wait until tomorrow
  • If severity is high or critical, coach dashboard shows an alert automatically without anyone triggering it
  • Alert shows the student's current situation, whether this is a recurring pattern or a first time, and what the coach should do next
  • If the situation is critical, a notification draft for the student's manager is included in the alert
Phase 4
Coach gets a prepared day
M7 Daily plan generated from signals
Completion
  • Coach triggers the plan and gets back a structured day
  • Every student who needs attention is slotted with a session type and a plain reason for why they are there today
  • Students who cannot fit into today are deferred to tomorrow with a reason
Phase 5
Coach walks into every meeting prepared
M8 Pre-meeting brief on any student
Completion
  • Coach requests a brief for any student and gets a focused summary
  • Brief covers the student's current academic situation, what has changed since the last session, any open concerns, and specific conversation starters for today
  • Brief draws from both factual memory and session summaries built in M5
Phase 6
Urgency changes the plan
M9 Serious concern updates the plan automatically
Completion
  • When a serious concern surfaces, the existing daily plan updates automatically
  • A new slot is added or someone is moved to tomorrow — every change has a clear reason attached
  • If two students are both critical and there is only one slot available, the system does not decide on its own — it surfaces both cases to the coach with the tradeoff clearly explained and asks the coach to make the call
  • Coach sees a summary of what changed in the plan and why before they even open the app
